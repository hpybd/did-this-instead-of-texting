IN NOMINE LUMINIS
To My Dearest Bengalibabe, 

The candles flicker low, much like the waning strength of my own spirit. You likely ponder the suddenness of my departure—why I choose to vanish into the mist before the appointed hour. Know this: I have come to realize that while we look to the Heavens for deliverance, the Almighty grants favor only to those who dare to grasp the reins of their own destiny. I have knelt until my knees were bruised; I have wept until the wells ran dry; I have sought anchor in both cold reason and fevered prayer. Yet, in the end, the silence of the Great Beyond pointed me back to my own two hands.

I leave this Cloister of Regulations to become a man unbound. To remain within these walls is to allow our bond to be choked by the dust of this wretched place. I refuse to let the "us" that breathes between us be tethered to this current misery. I seek a clean slate—one unblemished by the salt of old tears or the thorns of past misunderstandings.

Regarding our recent dance of words: I asked for a sign of your initiation, and though it arrived as a mere whisper—a shadow of a gesture—I have caught it. I accept it. But I pray you, have patience. Why must you strike at my heart with such sharp provocations simply to see if I still bleed? You seek a response, yet you use the very tools that drive me into the dark.

Understand my constitution: in the grand dramas of life, I have never been the protagonist. I am he who stands in the wings, the perennial shadow, the "second lead" of a play I did not write. When I recoil in anger, do not mistake it for malice; it is the reflexive shield of a man who has been sidelined until his very soul is calloused. It is a heavy task for me to believe that I—of all men—could truly be chosen.

Let these words be etched in the wax of this seal: I am consumed by love for thee. I shall remain thus, whether thy heart answers mine or remains silent. On the very day I first sent word to you, I vowed to my own soul that you would be my final horizon.

Grant me the grace to return to you at my own pace. Let me see my examinations to their end without further tempest. My body is currently a frail vessel, beset by sickness and a weariness that reaches the marrow. Do not tempt fate with idle whims or further hurt.

I remain, eternally and faithfully, thine,
